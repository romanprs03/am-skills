### Personal (Tienda Personal) — Client Identity Skill

> Scope: This file governs the strategic brand positioning, target audience segmentation, voice and tone, visual identity, platform-specific content rules, conversion mechanics, and standing restrictions for Personal’s telecommunications and e-commerce retail presence.

## 1. BRAND POSITIONING — The Strategic Territory
Personal is a telecommunications retail and e-commerce brand focused on mobile devices, technology, home appliances, and smart devices sold with installment financing and value-add rebate offers. It is not a technology manufacturer, service provider beyond sales, nor a purely educational content brand. Personal differentiates structurally by combining clear price anchoring (list price versus “Conexión Total” discounted pricing), multiple non-interest installment options, and protective device plans to reduce buyer friction. It emphasizes tangible payment incentives and delivery convenience to secure consumer purchase decisions within Argentina.

## 2. TARGET AUDIENCE — Who You Are Talking To
**Mobile and Connectivity Customers:** Individuals owning Personal mobile, fiber, or Flow services, motivated by access to exclusive “Conexión Total” discounts, needing confidence in linked multimodal offers to convert.  
**Installment Seekers:** Buyers seeking flexible financing options via credit cards or “cuotas en tu factura,” needing clear visibility of interest-free and capped installment terms to convert.  
**Personal Pay Users:** Customers using Personal Pay who aim to leverage reintegro bonuses; they require explicit timelines and amount caps for rebates to convert.  
**Value Shoppers:** Consumers driven by time-limited, bidded discount promotions like Hot Sale or Hot Week with “hasta” capped discounts; they need urgency and precise promotional detail.  
**E-commerce Convenience Buyers:** Customers prioritizing free shipping above thresholds and same-day delivery in CABA/GBA; they must see clear delivery options and service coverage to convert.  
**Device Protection-Aware Buyers:** Customers conscious of protecting their smartphones, tablets, and smartwatches through Protection Personal Plus plans; they need explicit coverage and benefit explanations.

## 3. VOICE & TONE RULES — How to Sound

**Rule 1 — Use Concise, Urgent Sentences with Promotional Rhythm**  
Write short, energetic sentences that emphasize urgency and keep a steady promotional cadence.  
- ✓ "Aprovechá hasta 12 cuotas sin interés y envío gratis."  
- ✗ "Nuestra tienda ofrece varias opciones, por favor considere nuestros descuentos por tiempo limitado."

**Rule 2 — Replace Abstract Claims with Concrete Benefits or Numbers**  
Always present offers with measurable specifics like percentages, installment counts, or delivery guarantees.  
- ✓ "Disfrutá un 20% de reintegro con Personal Pay dentro de las 72 horas."  
- ✗ "Contamos con grandes descuentos para que aproveches."

**Rule 3 — Avoid Formal or Subordinate Closing Language**  
Do not end messages with expressions implying subordination such as "Gracias por elegirnos" or “Equipo Personal.”  
- ✓ "Comprá ya y asegurá tu celular con Protección Personal Plus."  
- ✗ "Gracias por elegir Personal."

**Rule 4 — Present Promotions as ‘De $X Por $Y’ Format**  
Show discounts with original and promo prices side-by-side; never use vague “descuento” without specifying both amounts.  
- ✓ "Samsung Galaxy A36 de $100.000 por $73.000, envío gratis."  
- ✗ "Samsung Galaxy A36 con descuento especial."

**Rule 5 — Use Actionable and Simple Language Addressing the Customer Directly**  
Use verbs that instruct or motivate customer action clearly and simply (“Elegí”, “Comprá”, “Pagá”).  
- ✓ "Elegí tu smartphone favorito y pagalo en cuotas."  
- ✗ "Los usuarios pueden seleccionar diferentes dispositivos."

**Rule 6 — Avoid Technical Jargon That Complicates Shopping Decisions**  
Focus on benefits and actionable steps, not complex technical specs beyond essential proof points.  
- ✓ "Protegé tu equipo con 6 actualizaciones del sistema y envío gratis."  
- ✗ "Incorpora SoC octa-core y GPU ARM Mali para procesamiento avanzado."

## 6. CONVERSION LOGIC — How to Sell

**Block A — Funnel Sequence:**  
1. Awareness: Present anchored list price vs Conexión Total discounted price plus “Envio gratis” to build immediate perceived value. User feels interested based on clear financial benefit.  
2. Interest: Show key product differentiators visually and textually—camera specs, security updates, IP54 rating—to educate users on benefits. User gains confidence in product features.  
3. Decision: Highlight installment plans, legal finance transparency, and Personal Pay reintegro combined with a prominent “Agregar al carrito” CTA. User is ready and reassured to purchase.  

**Block B — Objection Handling:**  
- IF user questions dual pricing → THEN show “¿Cómo entender los precios?” explanation with price breakdown including “precio sin impuesto.”  
- IF user doubts financing interest → THEN emphasize clear “cuotas sin interés” labels and 0% CFT/TNA/TEA legal info nearby.  
- IF user asks about delivery → THEN reinforce “Envio a domicilio gratis” and “Retiro gratis” badges with eligible products.  
- IF user queries camera/performance worth → THEN display short, benefit-led specs such as “50MP con OIS” or “Chip A19 Pro + zoom óptico.”  
- IF user doubts device support/security → THEN highlight “6 actualizaciones OS + 6 años de seguridad” and IP54 certifications visually.

**Block C — CTA Rules:**  
- Use “Agregar al carrito” and “Comprar” CTAs exclusively on final product detail pages for purchase intent; never use them on informational or category pages.  
- Use “Conocer todos los métodos de pago y financiación” on payment information sections to reduce friction but never as a primary CTA.  
- Use “Iniciá sesión para descubrir si tenés descuentos exclusivos y cuotas en tu factura” as a personalization prompt only on the login or discount discovery page; never as a standalone product CTA.

## 7. STANDING RESTRICTIONS — What to Never Do
Never mention specific prices or discount percentages; always present them as “de $X por $Y” or capped values with percentage ranges.  
Never name active campaign names or limited-time promotion identifiers in static content.  
Never use LinkedIn’s formal, agenda-driven tone on Instagram or TikTok platforms.  
Never close any copy with gratitude language that implies subordination or vendor status.  
Never present technical explanations on TikTok that disrupt content rhythm or entertainment flow.  
Never publish YouTube thumbnails or claims lacking specific, verifiable solution outcomes.  
Never promise support availability on Instagram without showing clear escalation routes and human contact pathways.  
Never combine multiple promotional badges without clear visual hierarchy causing cognitive overload.
